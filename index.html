About

I am a PhD student at École de technologie supérieure (ÉTS) in Montreal, Canada, conducting research in AI for Software Engineering and Agentic AI. My work investigates how AI agents generate, modify, and maintain source code — and why human developers accept or reject these automated contributions.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I served as Head of Computer Center Department and Lecturer at the University College of Applied Sciences (UCAS) in Gaza, Palestine, where I led digital transformation initiatives and taught courses in Software Engineering, Database Systems, and Programming.

I hold an M.Sc. in Data Science from the Islamic University of Gaza, where my thesis evaluated Large Language Models for Automated Essay Scoring. My research interests span AI for SE, Agentic AI, LLMs, Prompt Engineering, and Educational Data Mining.
